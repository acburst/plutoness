EVEN WATER CAN KILL YOU

“Even Water Can Kill You” is a video that features excerpts from an improvisational experimental musique concrete/new classical yamaha keyboard album I recorded. It was screened as part of an installation that was part of an immersive show, First I Was Afraid… at Treasure Town, an arts collective in Clinton Hill, Brooklyn. It demonstrates my interest in experimental music forms, improvisation, and video. The video explores water as an analog for a source of artistic inspiration, as well as the Ouroboros as a metaphor for the point at which the most internal flips back out into the widest reach of the universe. The full album, Movie Music, features noise, poetry, musique concrete, and new classical improvisational keybaord music, and is a concept album about healing childhood trauma.
