This room is an exploration of a specific kind of nihilism.

The universe is a mess of infinite possibility
spilling like stuffing out the seams of a tightly sewn surface.
The sudden awareness of this profuse randomness and whimsy

can trigger paralyzing anxiety.
The responsibility to navigate
such a confusion of abundance
falls squarely on the individual.
It's enough to make you sick
your own authority over your choices and your attention.

A bedroom is a comfort zone and a symbol
of the most internal ring of the emotional life and psyche.
It's a place where you can enter chaos safely on an intimate scale.
To begin with the most basic of motions,
to grope in the dark for the unshakeable bricks of self
leads to a growing sense of how
your own naturalness
can serve as a guide
through the hopelessness of psychedelic opulence.

EVEN WATER CAN KILL YOU explores

the ataxic first steps into entropy
shyness and solitude
music and its relation
to the warmth of the internal world

but also the strange sense of confinement within yourself
the lonesomeness of your own imagination

sculpture, music, video + performance
VIEWERS ARE ENCOURAGED to lie on a bed
to watch the entirety of this video,
which is projected on the patterned tin ceiling
like a distorted astral projection of a dreaming body.

The video is played on loop,
the soundtrack imposing mood on the entire room.
