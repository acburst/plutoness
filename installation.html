this room is an exploration of a specific kind of nihilism.

the universe is a mess of possibility, spilling like stuffing out the seams of
a tightly sewn surface. the sudden awareness of this profuse randomness and whimsy can trigger paralyzing anxiety. at times it's enough to make you sick,
your own apparent authority over your choices and your attention.

a bedroom is a comfort zone and a symbol of the most internal ring of the emotional life and psyche. it's a place where you can enter chaos safely on an intimate scale. to begin with the most basic of motions, to grope in the dark for the unshakeable bricks of self leads to a growing sense of how your own naturalness can serve as a guide

through the hopelessness of psychedelic opulence. EVEN WATER CAN KILL YOU explores

the ataxic first steps into entropy
shyness and solitude
music and its relation
to the warmth of the internal world
but also the strange sense of confinement within yourself
the lonesomeness of your own imagination

VIEWERS ARE ENCOURAGED to lie on a bed
to watch the entirety of this video,
which is projected on the patterned tin ceiling
like a distorted astral projection of a dreaming body.

The video is played on loop,
the soundtrack imposing mood on the entire room.
